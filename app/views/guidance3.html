Finding the correct SIC code

{% set codesearch %}

Use our search tool to Find your standard industrial classification (SIC) code

Select a SIC code(s) that is the closest match to your business activity, it does not have to be an exact match.

To help you find the correct code, try these search suggestions:

  • Describe the goods or services the business produces or sells, for example ‘clothing retailing’ not ‘retailing’.
  • If you are self-employed, describe what you do, for example ‘dairy farming’ not ‘dairy farmer’.
  • Check that the keywords are spelt correctly.
  • Try different keywords or variations.

If you are self-employed:

  • The business activity relates to what you do to earn an income, not what the business you may work for does. For example, if you are a graphic designer who works for a large technology business, your activity is ‘graphic design’.

If you own a business:

  • The business activity relates to the goods sold or produced or the services you provide to your customers. For example, if you run a gardening business, but also have staff that do administration, the service your business provides is ‘gardening’ and the activity of the business is ‘gardening’.

If you are a shareholder employee:

  • The business activity relates to the goods sold or produced or the services provided by the business that employs you. For example, if you are providing accounting services as a shareholder employee of a textile manufacturing business, your activity is ‘textile manufacturing’.
